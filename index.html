The ground state of the helium atom (this is for test)

The Hamiltonian of a helium atom can be written as

$$ H(\textbf{r}_1,\textbf{r}_2)={{{\textbf{p}_1}^2} \over {2}} + {{{\textbf{p}_2}^2} \over {2}} - {2 \over r_1}-{2 \over r_2}+{1 \over |\textbf{r}_1-\textbf{r}_2|}$$ where we have written in the atomic units. The wavefunction for the space part of the ground state of the helium atom can be written as $$ \psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})=\psi_{1s}(\textbf{r$_1$};Z^*)\psi_{1s}(\textbf{r$_2$};Z^*)$$ in which \(\psi_{1s}(\textbf{r};Z^*)\) has a form of $$ \psi_{1s}(\textbf{r};Z^*)=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4\pi}}{Z^*}^{3/2}2e^{-Z^*r} $$ where \(Z^*\) is the effective number of charges of the nucleus. The energy of such state can be given by $$ E_0 =E_0(Z^*) = \int_\infty \psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})^*H\psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})dV_1dV_2 = -2\times \frac{1}{2}{Z^*}^2+ \int_\infty \psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})^*(\frac{1}{|\textbf{r$_1$}-\textbf{r$_2$}|}-\frac{2-Z^*}{r_1}-\frac{2-Z^*}{r_2})\psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})dV_1dV_2 =(Z^*)^2-\frac{27}{8}Z^*.$$ By the variation theory, we should have $$ \frac{\partial E_0}{\partial Z^*}=0 $$ which yields that \(Z^*=\frac{27}{16}\) from which we can infer that \(E_0= -2.85\) a.u. which corresponds to \(- 77.52\) eV.

Under perturbation theory, the zeroth order is given by

$$ H^{(0)}=-2\times\frac{1}{2}\times 2^2=-4 $$ and the first correction order is given by $$ H^{(1)}=\int_\infty \psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})^*\frac{1}{|\textbf{r$_1$}-\textbf{r$_2$}|}\psi_0(\textbf{r$_1$},\textbf{r$_2$})dV_1dV_2 $$ with \(Z^*=2\). After some calculations, the result is \(H^{(1)}=\frac{5}{4}\). Thus the energy of the ground state can be calculated as (to the first order) \(E_0=H^{(0)}+H^{(1)}=\frac{11}{4}\) a.u. which corresponds to \(-74.8\) eV.
