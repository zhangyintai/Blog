Make an AWG from newbie


Day 1 (April 8th, 2019)

Q: What is an AWG?
A: AWG is the abbreviation of arbitrary waveform generator. Thus, an AWG is an electronic device that can generate an arbitrary waveform.

Q: Why an AWG?
A: In the iontrap experiments, we need to change the position of the ions (which includes the separation of the ions and the transportation of the ions) adiabatically or fast-and-cold. This requires very accurate control of the electric potential distribution. Thus, we need to control the voltages that apply on the electrodes precisely. A digital-analog converter (DAC) can also be used to do such work probably, but it is often neither accurate nor fast enough comparing to the oscillation of the ions. Mostly the DACs are used for some rough operations, e.g. loading the ions. In addition, moving ions fast-and-cold can decrease the excitations and save time, both of the benefits make the realization of the quantum computers possible. Such protocols are impossible for DACs to perform while the AWG available.

Some other proper nouns:
FPGA: FPGA stands for field-programmable gate array.
LVDS: LVDS is the abbreviation of low-voltage differential signaling. It is a technique that was first introduced in 1994 to reduce the electromagnetic noise and the interference in the power and ground lines during transmission. What's more, as the name suggested, LVDS is unsensitive to the common mode voltage changes.
